Who We Are

About Memphis Cartoneras

📚 Our Mission

Memphis Cartoneras is a cooperative publishing house dedicated to nurturing literacy and creative expression among students through the art of handmade bookmaking. Using reclaimed and reused cardboard, our students don't just read books, they build them.

Every book produced in our workshops is a one-of-a-kind artifact: a testament to imagination, sustainability, and the transformative power of storytelling. We believe that when students hold a book they have made with their own hands, something extraordinary happens.

🌿 What We Stand For

✂ The Cartonera Tradition

The cartonera movement began in Buenos Aires in the early 2000s by writers and artists Washington Cucurto, Javier Barilaro, and Fernando Laguna. It started as a grassroots response to economic crisis and cultural hunger, aimed at promoting literacy, affordable literature, and sustainability.

Memphis Cartoneras carries that spirit forward: rooted in Memphis, connected to a global tradition of community publishing, and committed to making literary creation an act of joy and resistance.

How We Got Here

Our Origins & Journey

The Beginning

The Spark in Memphis

Memphis Cartoneras was founded by a Professor Elizabeth Pettinarolli at Rhodes College with a deep belief that storytelling belongs to everyone. Inspired by Latin American cartonera cooperatives, the idea was to bring handmade, community-published books to students in Memphis, a city with rich literary and musical traditions that often go unrecorded and unpublished.

Growing Roots

First Workshops & Books

The first cohort of student fellows gathered around a table covered in cardboard, scissors, glue, and paint. They wrote stories. They cut covers. They bound pages together with their own hands. The result was a small run of handmade books that circulated through classrooms and community spaces.

Expanding

A Community Takes Shape

What started as a small workshop grew into a cooperative with multiple cohorts of fellows, community partners, and a growing library of student-made books. Memphis Cartoneras began hosting public readings, bookmaking workshops for K–12 students, and events celebrating local voices.

Today

A Living, Growing Press

Memphis Cartoneras continues to grow, publishing new works each semester, training new fellows, and connecting students to a global tradition of grassroots literary publishing. Every book we make is a piece of Memphis history, crafted by the people who live it.

Meet the Founder

Professor Pettinarolli

Add photo

Elizabeth Pettinarolli

Department of Modern Languages · Rhodes College

Professor Pettinarolli founded Memphis Cartoneras out of a conviction that literary creation belongs to everyone — not just those with access to traditional publishing. Drawing on her scholarship in Latin American literature and her deep ties to the cartonera movement in Buenos Aires, she brought the cooperative bookmaking model to Memphis and built it into a living community press.

Scholarship & Research

Her academic work centers on grassroots publishing, Latin American literature, and the politics of literacy — the intellectual foundation Memphis Cartoneras is built on.

Fellowship Design

Professor Pettinarolli created and continues to shape the Fellows program — the curriculum, the workshops, the community partnerships, and the publishing process.
